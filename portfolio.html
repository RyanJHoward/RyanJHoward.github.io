Firefighting Drone

Designing the propulsion system of a drone to fight fires in urban centers

Course: AAE 339

Design Goals: Our five-person team designed a drone intended to fight fires at upper stories of urban buildings. We were inspired by this design.
Outcome: We selected a hybrid-electric propulsion system because the drone will spend most of it's time in hover, making electric power an excellent choice, but may also require significant power to manuever, therefore making hybrid the best suited power source. Another benefit of a hybrid-electric power system is the redundancy. We chose to contain the power supply on the firetruck and wire the energy supply to the drone, rather than containing the energy subsystems on the drone for safety and to lower the weight the drone needs to carry.

eVTOL Hexacopter

Passenger-Capable. All-Electric. Revolutionary.

Group: Vertical Flight Systems Purdue

Design Goals: Our long-term goal is to design an eVTOL air taxi competitive with industry leaders. Our first iteration, shown here, was to test our propulsion and avionics systems and gauge our manufacturing capabilities. For our next iteration, which is in the conceptual design phase, we are focusing on the following constraints.
Simplicity; as a student org with a limited budget, our design choices are driven by simplicity in design and manufacturing. Passenger-capable; we are passionate about Urban and Regional Air Mobility and are designing vehicles that goes beyond package delivery.

Challenges: When I was Structures Lead, we had finalized our design and were ready to begin purchasing material and manufacturing. When we spoke with a manufacturing expert we learned that Aluminum 6061 is very difficult to weld for novices - very brittle and fails easily. I led the redesign instead using Steel 4130 and by thinning the round tubing outer diameters, I saved both weight and cost.
Before I was elected president our club had no funding. In my first semester as president, I raised over $10k through grants and immediately begin purchasing materials and supplies to begin manufacturing.

Roles: President, Structures Team Lead, Structures Member

Processes Employed: SolidWorks CAD and Analysis, Vibrata, MIG welding

Website: https://sites.google.com/view/purduevfs/home

Optimizing Hexacopter Rotor Arm Thickness

Applied Optimization Methods to eVTOL Vehicle Design

Course: AAE 550: Multidisciplinary Design Optimization

Design Goals: Utilized optimization methods learned in the course and applied to the structural design of the Vertical Flight Systems eVTOL vehicle. For the vehicle design, we did not know how thick to make the inner and outer diameters of the rotor arm tubing, so we iterated through designs and checked our work with analysis. Also, the structure is too complex to solve for the optimal solution with simple hand-calcs. So, I used Sequential Quadratic Programming and Pareto curves to solve for the optimal solution and compared two materials.

Lunar Coring Drill

Design, Build, Test

Course: AAE 418: Zero-G Flight Experiment

Design Goals: Design a Stabilizing Jig, Coring Bit, and Containment Mechanism for use on the lunar surface. The design must also function underwater, because it will be tested at NASA's Neutral Bouyancy Lab (NBL) before, possibly, going to the moon. There are a number of other criteria for dimensions, weight, temperature, and operating conditions.

Results: I worked on Analysis, Design, and Outreach. I modeled parts in SolidWorks and performed FEA to validate the design. I spoke to a class of 9th graders about our project and answered their questions about space and engineering, and also developed our website. I learned how to CAM and use CNC waterjet and mill equipment to manufacture the stabilizing jig. We worked with contract manufacturers to We successfully tested our lunar coring drill at NASA's NBL facility in June.

Processes Employed: SolidWorks, Milling, Waterjet, 3D Printing, HTML, Github

Website: https://Purdue-NASA-NExT.io/
